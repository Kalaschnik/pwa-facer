PWA Facer

A Progressive Web App (PWA) utilizing Microsoft’s Face API
About

This project was part of the class Knowledge- and Content Management during winter term 18/19 in the department of Natural Language Processing, held by Prof Dr Gerhard Heyer & Dr Thomas Efer at Leipzig University. This project consists of two components. (1) A Progressive Web App, which enables the user to take and store photos (of faces to fulfill the purpose), and (2) the capability to analyze faces in regards to various aspects, such as: Age, Emotions, Gender, Pose, Smile, and Facial Hair, etc. I was particular interested in interpretation of emotions. Thus, only the basic emotions defined by Ekman[1] (i.e., Rage, Anger, Contempt, Disgust, Fear, Joy, Happiness, Sadness and Surprise) will be presented in the output.

Usage
When starting the first, the app shell will get cached. That means, as of now, when you reading these lines, this app works fully offline (apart from sending the data to the server).

Analyze facial expressions is easy, as long as a face can be detected on the photo. Just press the top left center_focus_strong button on a photo, and wait a few seconds. A snackbar at the bottom will pop up for 5 seconds with the corresponding emotion evaluation.

Taking photos. When you click the add button you’ll get asked to allow (1) Adding the site on your homescreen (recommended), which will "install" the website on your homescreen, (2) granting access to take pictures (recommended, yet if you don’t allow it an image picker will apear), and (3) by tapping get location, the browser will ask you accessing this information as well. Additionally, you can also opt in for Notifications and Push Messages. Then you get alerted when you (or somebody else) was uploading a picture.

[1]: Ekman, Paul (1999), Basic Emotions, in Dalgleish, T; Power, M (eds.), Handbook of Cognition and Emotion (PDF), Sussex, UK: John Wiley & Sons
